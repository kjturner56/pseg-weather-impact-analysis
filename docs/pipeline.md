# Pipeline Design

- **Load EIA Data:** Reads `pjm_daily_2025.csv` from the `data/` folder and loads it into a Pandas DataFrame for processing.
- **Load NOAA Data:** Reads `newark_weather_2025.csv` from the `data/` folder and loads it into a Pandas DataFrame for processing.
- **Clean EIA Data:** Converts the Date field to a standard datetime format, converts numeric fields to the appropriate data types, validates Demand, Forecast, Generation, and Interchange values, and outputs a cleaned EIA DataFrame.
- **Clean NOAA Data:** Converts the Date field to a standard datetime format, converts weather measurements to numeric values, calculates Average Temperature (TAVG) from TMAX and TMIN, checks for missing values, and outputs a cleaned NOAA DataFrame.
- **Merge Datasets:** Joins the cleaned EIA and NOAA datasets on the Date field to produce `merged_data.csv`.
- **Generate Metrics:** Calculates monthly demand, forecast error, weather-demand correlations, comparative statistics, and at least one normalized metric to support the research question. Outputs metrics tables for reporting.
- **Create Visualizations:** Generates labeled charts and figures from the merged dataset and saves them to the `output/` folder.
- **Final Orchestration:** `final_project.py` executes the complete pipeline, integrates all components, and produces the final datasets, metrics, and visualizations.

## Dashboard Decision

A simple interactive dashboard will be attempted as a stretch goal. The dashboard will consume the outputs generated by the analysis pipeline (metrics and visualizations) rather than recomputing them. If a fully functional dashboard cannot be completed, the development process, prompts used, and lessons learned will be documented as part of the project reflection.

## Normalized Metric

At least one normalized metric will be calculated after exploratory analysis of the merged dataset. The metric will be selected to best support meaningful comparison across weather conditions and time periods while aligning with the project's research question and course requirements.
